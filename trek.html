Trek

An app for outdoor travelers post pandemic

The application helps it’s users travel outdoors post pandemic. Dedicated to its users safety and supports them in day to day outdoor activities to planned travel by providing essential information about safety restrictions, location health, and visitor restrictions.

The Problem

People show up to parks only to find out that the park has visitor and pandemic restrictions not listed easily online.

The Solution

Booking app for outdoor experiences that focuses on pandemic related information such as capacity requirements or mask mandates.

Impact

75%

Of users felt more comfortable traveling to outdoor locations using an app like this.

50%

Increase in usability testing conversion rates from paper to high fidelity prototype.

Hypothesis

We believe that providing essential pandemic information for outdoor travelers will allow them to feel more comfortable and connected when they book through the app.

Prioritization of Solutions to Emily’s and User’s Pain Points

  • Trek should be clear and concise in what it offers. Too much information can be overwhelming and confusing.
  • The solution should include safety information to help alleviate stress surrounding the pandemic.
  • Relevant Imagery

Final Thoughts

More extensive research would assist in this case study dramatically and help inform the design process. Primary research, ethnographic research and marketing research tied to the user testing.

More of a focus on competitor analysis:
When I came up with Trek I thought it was a genius idea for an application that could find a place in a world post pandemic only to find several different applications that did exactly what I was thinking. This really challenged me to “stand out” and understand that there’s almost always going to be other creators with the same idea, but it’s up to the UX/UI process to inform a good app that people actually want to use.
