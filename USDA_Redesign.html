USDA Redesign

A fresh redesign of the
current USDA website

The United States Department of Agriculture, also known as the Agriculture Department, is the federal executive department responsible for developing and executing federal laws related to farming, forestry, rural economic development, and food

The Problem

The USDA website overall is confusing, filled with copious amounts of text, and filled with non-responsive design. Although a government agency, the USDA is lacking a consistent brand identity.

The Solution

Establish the brand identity as being trustworthy, organic, fresh, and informative for the USDA through responsive, consistent design pattern, relevant imagery, and more intuitive navigation/information layout.

Impact

53.3%

Increase in task success rate page navigation and accessing USDA resources

What is Grant’s Story?

Grant visits the USDA website for a variety of reasons-- including to apply for a farm ownership loan from the USDA, to find services and resources for farmers, ranchers, and producers, and to learn farm sustainability practices.

We established a user flow for our testing with this in mind.

  • Apply for a farm ownership loan.
  • Navigate to the Farmers.gov homepage
  • Navigate to the Conservation sub-page

Section 3. Design

How to design within the USDA guidelines with users in mind?

UI Style Direction

We want the new USDA brand to reflect the products our users provide for the American public: fresh, organic agricultural products, grown and raised in the USA. We’ll highlight this through the use of nature-based colors and imagery, as well as modernizing the graphics and website design to show that the USDA is a modern, high functioning government entity.

The colors for the current USDA site don’t seem to be contributing to establishing the brand identity proposed in their strategic goals. But how do we know what colors to use?
